\chapter{The Alignment Attractor}
\label{ch:alignment-attractor}

\begin{chapterthesis}
\textbf{[STUB]} Alignment is selected or destroyed by socio-technical basin dynamics; the relevant governance object is a pivotal process that shifts deployment from a race basin to a certified-deployment basin---not a single unilateral pivotal act.
\end{chapterthesis}

\section{Why This Matters}

The manuscript does not concede that a unilateral pivotal act is necessary.
It concedes only that some basin-shifting transition is necessary before unsafe superintelligence deployment.
The relevant object is not one decisive act, but a transition from a race basin to a certified-deployment basin.

\section{Plain-Language Model}

\textbf{[STUB]} Explain race basins, certified-deployment basins, and institutional pathways that shift selection pressure without requiring one actor to seize all power in a single move.

\section{Formal Model}

\textbf{[Defined]} A pivotal process is a socio-technical basin transition:
\[
\mathcal{B}_{\text{race}}
\to
\mathcal{B}_{\text{certified deployment}}.
\]

An alignment attractor is a self-stabilizing region where value-bundle geometry, bearer maps, correction-channel capacity, and successor constraints remain human-correctable under pressure.

\section{Worked Example}

\textbf{[STUB]} A certification regime that changes deployment incentives across labs without a single decisive takeover event.

\section{Counterexample or Failure Mode}

\textbf{[STUB]} A basin transition that requires de facto unilateral control while appearing as distributed certification---still a pivotal act in disguise.

\section{What Would Change This View}

\textbf{[STUB]} Evidence that race dynamics are self-correcting without explicit basin engineering, or that certified-deployment basins cannot form under realistic multipolar incentives.

\section{Summary}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{[Defined]} Pivotal process as $\mathcal{B}_{\text{race}} \to \mathcal{B}_{\text{certified deployment}}$.
  \item \textbf{[Open]} Whether such a transition can occur without unilateral decisive action.
  \item \textbf{[Conjectural]} Alignment attractors as self-stabilizing correction-preserving regions.
\end{itemize}
