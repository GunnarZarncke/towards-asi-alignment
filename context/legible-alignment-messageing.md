Your primary objective is *legibility in Wei Dai’s sense*: after reading, a smart non-specialist should (a) correctly predict what could go wrong, (b) see why it matters *before* deployment, and (c) know what concrete actions/artifacts would reduce risk—*without* needing to adopt your full ontology.

Hard constraints (legibility-first):

- Every core term must be either (i) defined operationally, or (ii) replaced with an ontology-light paraphrase.

- Make the intervention space explicit, not just the problem: propose artifacts, metrics, and mechanisms that fit current incentives.
- Include counterexamples and failure modes; highlight where the argument is shaky and what evidence would update you.
