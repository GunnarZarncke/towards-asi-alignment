## Chapter theses (brief)

**Ch38 — Checking a System at Every Level:** The alignment-relevant agent is whichever scale closes prediction, control, memory, selection, and correction into a stable loop; evaluators should keep a posterior over decompositions rather than fix one unit of analysis too early.

**Ch39 — A Safety Case for Superintelligence Alignment:** *(Still a stub; thesis inferred from ch38 handoff, ch31 template, and WWCTV.)* Assemble prior tools into an explicit safety case—claims, evidence, assumptions, stop conditions—while acknowledging that a case can only discharge *imagined* failure modes unless metrics are adversarially verifiable.

**Ch39b — Verifiability and Ontology Adequacy:** Every measurand needs an adversarial-verifiability label; ontology gaps mostly reduce to detection; when direct reading fails, bound the *cost of faking*—or fall back to proof (GSAI) or non-deployment.
