### Verification notes

1. **Heraclitus (Ch08 Originating):** Fragment B12 is attested; the famous "cannot step twice into the same river" wording comes via Plato/Plutarch, not the fragment itself. The table uses the safer primary fragment.
2. **Plutarch (Ch08 Illustrating):** Wording varies slightly by translation (Dryden/Perrin); the sense is stable across standard editions.
3. **Simon (Ch06 Reframing):** The exact phrasing appears in *Administrative Behavior* and is echoed in Simon's Nobel lecture; minor punctuation variants exist across editions.
4. **LeCun (Ch09 Contradicting):** Quote is from a podcast transcript; substance is consistent with his published interviews, but it is spoken rather than a peer-reviewed paper.
