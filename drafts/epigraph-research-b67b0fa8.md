### Verification notes

1. **Misattribution trap:** “People will come to love their oppression, to adore the technologies that undo their capacities to think” is **Neil Postman** (1985), summarizing Huxley—not Huxley verbatim. The Huxley “love their servitude” quote above is verified from the 1946 *Brave New World* foreword.
2. **Goodhart date:** The law is often cited to the 1984 book; Goodhart himself notes the remark dates to **1975**.
3. **ch44/ch43:** Quotes are chosen for **intended** chapter roles until those chapters are drafted.
4. **Parfit / Olson:** Both are real, standard citations; Olson’s line is paraphrased from his animalism argument—if you want a shorter epigraph, use: “I believe that we are animals” (Olson, *What Are We?*, 2007).
