### Notes on verification

- **Heraclitus B12** is attested via Plato's paraphrase; the exact English wording varies by translator (Kahn/Robinson: "different and different waters flow").
- **Ch29 Salient row**: The chapter's invariant-preservation line is authorial; the von Neumann quote is the closest classic analogue on successor reproduction without degeneration.
- **Ch30 Reframing row**: The Turing line is the best *published* analogue to private self-knowledge without overseer access; the chapter's explicit self-modeling/self-transparency distinction is original to the manuscript.
- **Delphic "Know thyself"** is a traditional inscription, not Socrates's own words (though he made it central).
- All **2010+ contemporary** rows are from named AI safety researchers with citable papers, books, or forum/podcast transcripts.
